Updated September 29, 2025

The OmniBlocks project respects your privacy. Every website says this, but we mean it: We aren't interested in your data. We genuinely have no reason to need your information, especially as our goal is to provide a free, open source IDE that everyone can use. In the interest of full transparency, this document lists every place where your data might be collected or shared:

Loading projects

OmniBlocks relies on TurboWarp to maintain compatibility with Scratch.

When you load a project from another website, you are subject to the privacy practices of that website. For example, when loading projects from Scratch, you are subject to the Scratch privacy policy. When loading projects from Scratch specifically, the project ID will also be shared with TurboWarp as part of Scratch's API does not allow direct access. We may briefly log the project ID for caching.

Running projects

When connecting to our cloud variable servers, the project ID and username may be logged for 14 days. Data in cloud variables is sent to anyone else connected at the same time. Custom cloud variable servers are outside of our control.

Some extensions must communicate with external APIs to function. For example, the translate and text-to-speech extensions rely on the Scratch API, which is covered by the Scratch privacy policy. To improve performance, some extensions access TurboWarp's APIs instead where both the request (for example, the text being translated) and the result after forwarding to Scratch (for example, the translated version of the text) may be cached.

Most extensions will ask for permission when the project attempts to use the extension to access an untrusted website, however we cannot guarantee this is 100% reliable.

When loading a custom extension from a place other than the official gallery such as a URL or file on your computer, the editor will ask for permission to load the extension. If you approve this dialog, the extension can bypass the permission dialogs that official extensions show.

Settings

Many OmniBlocks services will store settings such as addon settings or packager settings in your browser.

Updated September 27, 2025

The TurboWarp project respects your privacy. We're an open source project ran by volunteers. For transparency, this page lists all the places in TurboWarp where we or a third-party receive your data and how your data is handled.

Abuse

When a service is being abused, we may take measures to collect information to protect the service. For example, we could log IP addresses making excessive amounts of requests. We strive to do this carefully so that no one is caught in the crossfire. Data is kept only as long as needed to stop the abuse.

A randomly generated username may be saved in your browser. You can replace this with a custom username. Randomly generated usernames are anonymized (the random part is removed) before being sent to any cloud variable server.

OmniBlocks Desktop

OmniBlocks Desktop is being developed as of the last time this was updated.

OmniBlocks Packager

The OmniBlocks Packager may store settings and cache large downloads such as Electron in your browser for convenience and performance.

When packaging a project using custom extensions, the packager may access those websites to download the extensions.

Projects generated by the OmniBlocks Packager

Packaged projects include all scripts, costumes, and sounds to run the project. The packager tries to include all custom extensions too, but in some cases, the packaged project may need to download certain extensions each time it runs.

Advanced features such as third-party custom extensions, custom cloud host, custom JS, and custom CSS are outside of our control.

Note that, unlike the OmniBlocks editor, custom extensions in packaged projects will not ask for permission before contacting websites.

You are responsible for informing end users about the privacy practices of the files you distribute. Do not link to this document as your project's privacy policy as it may change in the future. You must write your own.

Future

OmniBlocks is planning to add new features as it develops into a full-fledged IDE. This means this policy will change as we add new features, but don't worry, as we still aren't interested in your data.

What to expect from planned updates and how it affects privacy

  • OmniBlocks BoxyVM: OmniBlocks is planning to add Multi-Language support, so you can make the programs of your dreams in a ton of languages, such as Python, C++, and more. This includes BoxyVM, a client-side VM that is used to run your code. If you have Internet access enabled on your Boxy, you are responsible for any internet requests, and the privacy of your data depends on those sites.

Contact

Any concerns related to privacy or any other matter should be discussed in the OmniBlocks Organization Discussions
